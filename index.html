Human Trafficking in America

I look into the human trafficking epidemic in America
and specific issues within human trafficking. I beleive young women should be at the forefront of combating sexual slavery.

Sexual slavery disproportionately affects young women; however, few young women are recruited to help combat sex trafficking in America. I hope that I can help inspire young women to join forces to stop sexual slavery. Human trafficking is often thought of as an issue that affects other countries, when in fact it is prevalent in the U.S as well. Along with this, many women who are trafficked are American citizens. In order to address sexual slavery in America, we must first look into how human trafficking works in America, who is affected, and potential preventative measures.

Get Involved in the Fight Against Trafficking

While reading about human trafficking in America is the first step for prevention, getting involve wth organizations fighting sexual slavery is hugely important. Below are some organizations that address human trafficking..

  • Rights4Girls

    • Rights4girls is an organization working to help minors in the slave trade, and fighting gender-based violence. The website features several ways to get involved, federally, locally, and personally. You can also donate to Rights4girls so they can continue their work.

  • Breaking Free

    • Breaking Free is based in Minneapolis, MN and works to help women out of trafficking at provide them an education and career outside of the sex industry. The organization also has a house for women who esacpe sexual slavery, and they help women in court if they have charges against them for being a sex slave. You can donate money, household supplies, and get involved in Minneapilis, among other things to help this organization.

  • With Two Wings

    • With Two Wings is based in Los Angeles, CA. Their main mission is to educate and train former sex slaves so they can pursue careers they want to, and to help them become financially secure. Ways to get involved are donating money, volunteering to work as a mentor or therapist, and applying to work as an intern.

  • The Human Trafficking Hotline Referral Directory

    • The Human Trafficking Hotline's website has a directory you can fill out to find local organizations and events working to stop sexual slavery. This tool can be very helpful due to the fact that it is often difficult to work with organizations not close to home. This is a great resouce to find opportunities for hands-on prevention.

  • Other ways to fight sexual slavery:

    • Search the internet to find other organizations to get involved with. Researching indicators that a woman is being trafficked is a great way to keep your eye out for signs a woman you know is being sold as a sex slave. Educate yourself and look into specific ways you can prevent trafficking within your community and social circles.
